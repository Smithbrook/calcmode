About Percentage Change

Percentage change is a mathematical concept that expresses the degree of change over time. It's calculated by finding the difference between the new and original values, dividing by the original value, and multiplying by 100.

This calculation is essential in many fields including finance, statistics, science, and business analysis. Whether you're tracking stock prices, analyzing sales growth, or measuring any kind of change over time, percentage change provides a standardized way to express that change.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between percentage change and percentage point change?
Percentage change shows the relative change as a percentage of the original value. Percentage point change shows the absolute difference between two percentages. For example, if something goes from 10% to 15%, that's a 50% change but a 5 percentage point change.
Can percentage change be negative?
Yes! A negative percentage change indicates a decrease. For example, if a value goes from 100 to 80, that's a -20% change, showing a 20% decrease.
How do I calculate percentage increase vs percentage decrease?
The formula is the same for both. If the new value is larger than the original, you get a positive percentage (increase). If the new value is smaller, you get a negative percentage (decrease).
What if the original value is zero?
You cannot calculate percentage change when the original value is zero, as this would involve division by zero. In such cases, you might consider using absolute change instead.
How is this useful in real life?
Percentage change is used everywhere: tracking investment returns, analyzing business growth, comparing prices, measuring performance improvements, scientific research, and much more. It provides a standardized way to express change regardless of the scale of the numbers involved.
